So what's inside a transformer model?

Lots of numbers, vectors, and matrices whose values were decided and frozen during training, which work together to make predictions based on the training data.

1 · What it learned

Just two patterns:

CA→T
BA→D

A real model will be trained on billions/trillions of words.

2 · Where each letter sits

A second small list marks whether a letter came first or second.

position 1[−0.22, 0.57]
position 2[−0.53, −0.15]
letter + position = what goes into the model

3 · A tiny vocabulary

The model knows only five letters. Each one is stored as a short list of numbers it learned — a "word-vector."

letter learned list of numbers
C[ 0.07, −1.14 ]
B[ 0.54, −1.00 ]
A[ 0.62, 1.54 ]
T[−1.62, −1.70 ]
D[−1.15, 2.91 ]

The numbers aren't hand-written meanings — training just found values that work.

4 · Other Math Inside

A
AttentionLets the latest letter look back at earlier ones and pull in the context that matters. This is the one genuinely new part.
×
Weighted mixingA few matrix multiplies — the same "blend the numbers" step from Travis, repeated.
+
A little housekeepingSmall steps that keep the numbers stable and sharpen the final guess.
About 42 learned numbers in total that is the entire model — during prediction it only looks them up and runs the arithmetic
