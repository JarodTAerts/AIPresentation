So how does the model's weight matrix get filled in?

Nobody types in a trillion numbers. They get learned from staggering amounts of text — by playing fill-in-the-blank, billions of times.

Step 01 · Gather
Collect the internet
Pull ~15+ trillion words of human text: web pages, books, Wikipedia, scientific papers, GitHub code, forum posts. Clean it, dedupe it, filter junk.
≈ 50,000× all of Wikipedia
Step 02 · Predict
Play fill-in-the-blank
Show the model a piece of real text with the next word hidden. Ask it to guess. That's it. That's the whole game.
"The cat sat on the ___" → mat
repeated trillions of times
Step 03 · Adjust
Nudge the weights
Wrong guess? Every number in the matrix gets bumped a tiny bit — in the direction that would have made the answer less wrong. Statistics, not understanding.
≈ trillions of tiny nudges
Step 04 · Refine
Polish with humans
After the base model knows language statistically, humans rate its answers (helpful? safe? honest?). The weights get nudged one more time to behave like an assistant, not an autocomplete.
RLHF + instruction tuning
⚡ Scale
A single frontier training run: ~tens of thousands of GPUs running weeks to months, costing $100M+. The "weights" are the leftover residue — every statistical pattern in human language, compressed into a trillion numbers.

Llama 3 trained on ~15T tokens · GPT-4 reportedly $100M+ compute · Modern frontier runs: 25K–100K+ H100/B200 GPUs for months.
