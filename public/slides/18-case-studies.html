Cuts, reversals, and the price of getting it wrong

Four recent cases show why “AI replaced jobs” is only half the story. Some firms cut deeply; others restored human expertise when quality or demand assumptions failed.

Oracle · June 2026 · SEC filing
21,000 fewer employees — AI explicitly cited

Workforce fell 162K → 141K. Oracle's 10-K says AI adoption “resulted…in reductions,” while warning about lost institutional knowledge and morale.

Also spent $1.8B restructuring while capex rose to $55.7B.
Ford · June 2026 · hybrid reversal
350 veteran engineers brought back into quality work

Ford said automated systems lacked tacit product-cycle knowledge. Veteran engineers were rehired, hired, or promoted — and used to improve the AI tools.

Ford then ranked #1 among mainstream brands in J.D. Power initial quality.
Commonwealth Bank · Aug 2025 · reinstatement
45 AI-linked cuts reversed after calls rose

A voice bot was expected to reduce demand. Staff reported overtime and higher call volumes; the bank restored every role and called its redundancy assessment an error.

A small but unusually well-documented reversal.
Cloudflare · May 2026 · displacement
1,100 roles cut during a record-revenue quarter

Cloudflare eliminated 20% of staff and called it an “agentic AI era” operating reset — while still reporting a quarterly net loss.

The company expects employment to grow again after the restructuring.

These are cases, not an average. One driver of cuts is the Software Brain effect from earlier — cheaper software lets one person cover more, so fewer are needed per task. But AI can also misread tacit knowledge or real demand, forcing costly reversals.

Sources: Oracle FY2026 10-K (Jun 22 2026) · Bloomberg/Ford and J.D. Power (Jun 25 2026) · ABC Australia/CBA (Aug 21 2025) · Cloudflare Q1 2026 earnings and 10-Q.
