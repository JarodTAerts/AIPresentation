hollowing out the bottom

The entry-level rung is the one shaking first.

% change in employment by age, AI-exposed occupations (2022 → 2025)

Brynjolfsson, Chandar & Chen — "Canaries in the Coal Mine?" Stanford Digital Economy Lab / NBER (Aug 2025)

The pattern

  • 22–25 year olds: −16% employment in AI-exposed jobs.
  • 26–30: −7%. 30+: roughly flat or positive.
  • Hits hardest in software, customer support, content writing, paralegal work, junior analyst roles.

Why juniors first?

Their tasks were codified, repeatable, and well-documented. That's exactly what current AI is good at. Senior judgment, novel problems, and stakeholder management — not yet.

The traditional career ladder assumed someone would pay juniors to learn. If the bottom rung disappears, how do we make seniors?
