Linear Algebra Review 101

What is linear algebra?

Linear algebra is just the math of lists and tables of numbers — and the rules for mixing them together.

Matrix multiplication does most of the heavy lifting in modern AI — repeated billions or trillions of times per response.

But it is one ingredient of AI, not the whole recipe: attention chooses what context to mix, while training data shapes the patterns available to use.

The vocabulary on the right is enough to start following the computation.

  • vectorA list of numbers — like [hunger 8, tired 3, mood 6]. A snapshot.
  • matrixA table of numbers. Each row is a "recipe."
  • multiplyA matrix × vector = weighted mixing. Each output is a blend of all the inputs.
