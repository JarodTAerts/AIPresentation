This isn't just charts — it's already in earnings calls.

Klarna

AI customer-service agent doing the work of ~700 full-time agents. Resolution times dropped from 11 min → under 2. Headcount fell from ~7,400 → ~3,400 (2022–2025).

Klarna Q2 2024 letter; Siemiatkowski interviews.

Amazon (AWS)

Andy Jassy memo, June 2025: "We will need fewer people doing some of the jobs that are being done today." Layoffs across corporate roles followed.

Amazon all-hands memo, June 17 2025.

Duolingo

"AI-first" reorg, May 2025. Contractors doing translatable work cut. Internal teams restructured around AI tooling.

Luis von Ahn internal memo, May 2025.

Big Law / Big 4

Several firms publicly reducing first-year associate / staff-consultant intake — Allen & Overy, Goldman Sachs, KPMG cited AI explicitly.

FT, Reuters, Bloomberg 2024–2025 coverage.

The honest caveat — some are re-hiring. Klarna's CEO admitted (May 2025) the AI-only push went too far on quality; they re-opened gig-style human roles. IBM's Krishna said ~200 HR jobs were replaced by AI while overall headcount grew — savings funded growth elsewhere. Net pattern: fewer entry-level seats, more senior leverage.
