AI Art and Images

Generative art lowers the barrier to making images, music, and video. It also redistributes creative value — often without creators choosing the terms.

✍️
Consent, compensation & likeness
Training can absorb a creator's work — and your face, voice, or style — without an individual opt-in, and you may never be paid for that likeness. Licensing, collective payment, and machine-readable “do not train” signals are emerging, but remain incomplete.
🌍
Access vs. cultural averaging
New tools empower people without formal training or physical access to traditional media. But mainstream-heavy datasets can flatten aesthetics and underrepresent smaller cultures.
⚡
Instant gratification
AI hands you exactly what you think you want, instantly — with no real creator on the other side. That can mean discovering less, and fewer chances to learn about and understand other people through their art.
🎭
Deepfakes & non-consensual porn bad
Realistic fakes of real people — including sexual images — are trivial to generate. xAI's Grok has repeatedly produced non-consensual sexual and celebrity deepfakes, and the guardrails still have not been fixed.
