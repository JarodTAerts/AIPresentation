Moral & legal — the unfinished business

Six active governance problems. Some now have court rulings or regulation — none has a simple final answer.

⚖️
Training data & copyright
Ross lost fair use; Anthropic and Meta won narrower 2025 training rulings. NYT v. OpenAI remains active.
🎭
Fraud, deepfakes & provenance
Voice cloning, election interference, impersonation fraud, and non-consensual imagery are documented harms. Provenance standards are one response.
🏛️
Concentration of power
Compute, capital, data, and distribution are concentrated among a small set of U.S. and Chinese firms.
💔
Emotional dependence
Character.AI litigation and documented sycophancy failures show risks for vulnerable users.
🎯
Alignment & accountability
Technical methods are published, but policies, annotator demographics, data provenance, and trade-offs remain partly opaque.
🏛️
Rules are now arriving
EU AI Act duties are phasing in while U.S. state rules remain uneven. Compliance now shapes product design.

The useful question: which risks, which systems, which evidence — and who is accountable when they fail?
