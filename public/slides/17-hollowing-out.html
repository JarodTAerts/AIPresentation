hollowing out the bottom

Early-career workers show the clearest decline.

Relative employment change after firm-level controls · cyan marker = broadly stable (~0%), not missing data

Stanford Digital Economy Lab / SIEPR working paper, Nov 2025 revision

The pattern

  • Ages 22–25: a 13–16% relative employment decline in the most AI-exposed occupations.
  • More experienced workers: employment in the same occupations remained broadly stable.
  • Data: ADP payroll records covering about 4.6M workers across 730+ occupations.
Caveat: this is a working-paper association, not a randomized experiment. Interest rates and hiring cycles remain competing explanations. The career-ladder question is serious — but not settled.
